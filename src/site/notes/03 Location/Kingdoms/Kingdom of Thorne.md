### 📜 The Life of the Manor

Thorne is the heartland of the 6 Kingdoms, serving as the baseline for the world. It is a place of rolling hills, fertile farmland, and dense green forests—the classic image of a high-fantasy realm.

#### 1. The Era of Equality

Thorne is defined by its social structure. Unlike the specialized kingdoms, Thorne was built on the principle of total integration.

- **The Population:** Humans, Dwarves, Elves, and Dragonborn live side-by-side without a hierarchy. It is a true melting pot where racial borders have faded into a shared culture of labor and community.
    
- **The Atmosphere:** It is a welcoming, bustling land. The towns are filled with thatched-roof cottages, stone bridges, and open-air markets that serve travelers from every corner of the map.
    

#### 2. The Generic Heartland

Thorne represents the "standard" way of life. It isn't a land of extreme mana-density or high-altitude spires; it is a land of soil and seasons.

- **Geography:** Vast plains and winding rivers. It provides the majority of the food and timber for the other kingdoms.
    
- **Transportation:** Well-maintained dirt roads and horse-drawn carriages. It is the central hub through which all land-based trade must pass.
    

#### 3. The Crossroads

Because it is so "central" and neutral, Thorne is the most frequent meeting place for diplomats and merchants. It doesn't have the intimidating obsidian walls of Oros or the confusing water-locks of Nyxos. In Thorne, a traveler is just another face in the crowd.
