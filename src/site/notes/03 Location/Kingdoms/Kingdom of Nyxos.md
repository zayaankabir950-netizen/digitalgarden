### 📜 The Life of the Manor

Nyxos is a land defined by its absolute isolation. It sits within a massive, sunken basin protected by a literal wall of endless, crashing waterfalls. The constant roar of the water creates a sensory barrier that masks the city from the outside world, making it the most secluded of the 6 Kingdoms.

#### 1. The Veil of Water

There are no roads, mountain passes, or bridges leading into Nyxos. The geography itself acts as an unbreakable fortress.

- **The Guided Entry:** The only way to penetrate the basin is to be "Fetched." A resident Navigator must exit the falls and guide an outsider's vessel through the specific, rhythmic patterns of the currents. Without a guide, any boat is simply crushed or pushed back by the sheer force of the descent.
    
- **The Atmosphere:** Once inside, the roar of the falls fades into a gentle mist. The air is cool and perpetually filled with rainbows, creating a serene, untouched beauty that feels disconnected from the industrial smoke of the other kingdoms.
    

#### 2. The City of Water

The capital is a masterpiece of aquatic engineering. Every "street" is a waterway, and the architecture is a seamless blend of Dwarven stonework and Human elegance.

- **Transportation:** Ornate gondolas and floating walkways are the only ways to move. The buildings are constructed from a pale, water-resistant stone that glows softly in the moonlight.
    
- **The Symbiosis:** The Dwarves handle the deep-water foundations and the massive "Lock" systems that keep the city afloat, while the Humans manage the surface navigation and the delicate trade of the basin's rare water-flora.
    

#### 3. The Rumor of Frey

While the rest of the world scrambles for power and mana-crystals, the scholars of Nyxos are rumored to be guarding something much older and more dangerous.

- **The Library of Frey:** Ancient whispers suggest that the entrance to the legendary **Library of Frey**—the ultimate repository of pre-mirror knowledge—is hidden somewhere deep beneath the city's central canal.
    
- **The Gatekeepers:** The people of Nyxos are seen as the "Keepers of the Record," though they rarely confirm if the library truly exists, preferring to let the rumors serve as a deterrent to the curious.
    

#### 4. The Vibe of the Land

Nyxos feels like a secret the world forgot to tell. It is a place of profound reflection and quiet. Visitors describe a feeling of weightlessness, as if the political friction and racial tensions of the **Era of Coexistence** simply cannot penetrate the wall of water.
