### 📜 The Life of the Manor

Valerius is a land of white marble pillars, open-air theaters, and ancient, towering forests. It feels like a dream of a golden age that never ended.

#### 1. The Pacifist Elves

The Elves of Valerius carry a deep, ancestral guilt from the **Age of Shien**. While the Merchant Empires were burning the world for crystals, the Valerian Elves retreated to their groves and swore an oath of non-violence.

- **Appearance:** They dress in flowing silks and simple tunics, often barefoot to keep a constant connection to the earth.
    
- **The Oath:** A Valerian Elf will not draw a blade. They believe that to take a life—even in defense—is to shatter the "Continuity" that House Nexum works so hard to protect.
    

#### 2. Nature and Architecture

They don't cut down trees to build cities; they grow the trees _into_ the cities.

- **The Marble Groves:** Their homes are a blend of living wood and polished white stone. They use magic to encourage vines to wrap around pillars, creating natural "halls" that smell of jasmine and rain.
    
- **The Agora:** Life happens in the sun. The Elves spend their days in long philosophical debates, studying the arts, and practicing the "supportive" magics taught at the academy.
    

#### 3. House Nexum (The 2nd House)

The **House of Healing and Support** is the heart of Valerian culture.

- **The Healer-Sages:** In Valerius, mages are seen as the "Keepers of the Grove." They don't just heal people; they heal the land itself if it becomes scarred by industrial runoff from the other kingdoms.
    
- **The Diplomacy:** Because they are pacifists, Valerius is often the location for peace talks between the Legacy Four. Nobody brings a weapon into Valerius—it’s considered the ultimate taboo.
    

#### 4. The Vibe of the Land

Traveling through Valerius is like walking through a living painting. There are no fortresses, no guard towers, and no prisons. Instead, there are "Sanctuaries"—open spaces where anyone can come to rest. The only "defense" they have is the thick, ancient magic of the woods that makes unwanted visitors feel a deep, overwhelming urge to just... turn around and go home.
