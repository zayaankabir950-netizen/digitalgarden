### 📜 The Chronicle of the Branch

The high-and-mighty mages of the current era don't like to admit it, but the foundation of their kingdom wasn't a grand ritual—it was a clumsy accident.

#### 1. The First Beam

During the early days of **The Age of Shien**, a nameless traveler (often depicted as a simple scout) tripped while crossing a mana-rich clearing. To catch his balance, he swung a dry branch through the air.

- **The Spark:** The movement accidentally "aligned" with a local mana-vein.
    
- **The Result:** A searing beam of pure energy shot from the wood, leveling a nearby tree.
    
- **The Discovery:** This proved that magic wasn't just for godlike beings—it could be triggered by anyone with the right movement.
    

#### 2. The Culture of Rules

Because their magic started with a specific physical motion, Aethelgarders are obsessed with **Form**.

- They believe magic is a series of "Laws" that must be followed perfectly to avoid disaster.
    
- Their cities are organized into perfect grids, and their society is heavily bureaucratic.
    
- **The Scholars:** They spend decades cataloging the "Swing" and "Flick" of every spell to ensure it never fails.
    

#### 3. [[04 Lore/Houses/House Magica\|House Magica]] (The First House)

Located in the heart of the capital, this academy is where the "Science of the Branch" is taught.

- Students spend years just learning how to stand and breathe before they are allowed to even touch a focus.
    
- It is the most prestigious and strict school in the 6 Kingdoms.
    

#### 4. The Human Element

As the primary race, humans in Aethelgard are the "Administrators" of the current peace. They authored the **Accord of Shards** and generally act as the world's librarians and lawyers. They are orderly and clean, but that order can feel cold to those who prefer the wilder magic of the newer kingdoms.
